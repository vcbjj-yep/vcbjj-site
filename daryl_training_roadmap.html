Why This Session Now
You have had North/South in your game since Session #84 — underhooks, cross face, kimura/Americana/armbar/triangle/back take were all introduced there. What was missing was the finishing mechanics layer: not which submissions are available, but how to get the tap from the pin when a resisting partner is defending. The Orphan Limb Connection
The open mat roll (Session #94) identified that orphan limbs — legs, hips, neck, head — were being isolated and used against you repeatedly without you recognising the pattern early enough. N/S finishing work is the direct answer: you learn to isolate your partner's limbs from N/S top, which makes you acutely aware of what it looks like and feels like from both ends.
The Principle From Session #94
Close the first link (legs) before defences fire. From N/S top, this translates: control the near arm and prevent hip movement before committing to the submission — otherwise you are attacking into a position your partner can still escape from. Same orphan limb principle, applied from the top.

Principles to Carry Into Every Session

Your Thinking Framework

When you are playing guard: your job is to destabilise.

Get your partner's hips or hands to the mat. Everything else — sweeps, submissions, back takes — flows from that. If you are not destabilising, you are stalling.

A failed attack is not a failure — it is information.

Session #88 was entirely built around failed triangles. Every counter your partner makes tells you which follow-up to use. You have four mapped already. Use them.

Know how you got there — not just how to escape.

From today: every pin escape must be practised from the transition into that pin. If you only drill the escape, you will never recognise the moment you need it. Recognising the position as it forms is half the battle.

Connection and disconnection are your language.

Every grip, every frame, every hook is either building or breaking a connection. When you are pulling, you are attacking. When you are pushing, you are defending. Know which one you are doing at all times.
