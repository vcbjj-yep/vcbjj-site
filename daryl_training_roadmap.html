Why these games matter for you specifically

Games 1–3: Single Limb Defense
You will use only one leg, then only one arm, then one leg and one arm — to stop your partner passing your hip line. No guard pulling, no sweeping. Pure survival. This exposes what your body does instinctively when stripped of its usual tools, and forces your hips to move efficiently.
Games 4–5: Staggered Legs and Knees to Chest
Staggered legs teach you how to cover multiple angles with one body shape. Knees-to-chest with both arms builds the defensive shell that buys you time to re-engage — the same position you need before re-establishing your K-Guard or shin-to-shin.
Game 6: Frame Recovery Under Pressure
You now add a frame arm to recover after your legs are pushed away. This is the connection point to everything you have been building — a guard is only useful if you can get back into it when things go wrong.
What to focus on today: Move perpendicular to your partner — your spine should stay at a right angle to their hip line as they move around you. This is the single most important habit from today's session. If your triangle attacks have been feeling late, this is why.

Principles to Carry Into Every Session

Your Thinking Framework

When you are playing guard: your job is to destabilise.

Get your partner's hips or hands to the mat. Everything else — sweeps, submissions, back takes — flows from that. If you are not destabilising, you are stalling.

A failed attack is not a failure — it is information.

Session #88 was entirely built around failed triangles. Every counter your partner makes tells you which follow-up to use. You have four mapped already. Use them.

Move perpendicular, not backwards.

When a passer moves into you, the instinct is to push back or retreat. Moving perpendicular to their hip line (from today's games) is what keeps you in a fighting position rather than a defensive one.

Connection and disconnection are your language.

Every grip, every frame, every hook is either building or breaking a connection. When you are pulling, you are attacking. When you are pushing, you are defending. Know which one you are doing at all times.
