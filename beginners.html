What This Is

Most people who start BJJ are dropped into a regular class on day one. They tap repeatedly, have no frame of reference for what just happened, and spend the first month trying to catch up on context everyone else already has.

This course inverts that. Eight weeks of Wednesday evenings, structured specifically for beginners — so that when you move into regular training, you already know the room.

Not a trial
This is a committed 8-week programme, not a sample session. You’re here to learn, not to observe.
Closed cohort
The same 6 people, every Wednesday. No rotating strangers, no disruption to the group dynamic.
Structured curriculum
Each session builds on the last. Nothing is random, nothing repeats. You leave every week knowing exactly what you added.
Designed for 40+
Pace is set for adult bodies that recover differently. Smart training, not grinding.
The 8 Weeks

Each session is 90 minutes: 15 minutes of movement prep, 50 minutes of technique and positional drilling, 25 minutes of controlled live practice.

Week 01
The Ground — Your New Normal
How to fall safely, how to move on the mat, and why BJJ starts from positions that feel completely unnatural. Orientation to the mat environment. You will tap this session. That is the point.
Breakfalls · Shrimping · Hip escape
Week 02
Position Before Submission
The core principle of BJJ hierarchy: why where you are matters more than what you’re trying to do. Introduction to dominant positions — mount, side control, back control — and why some positions are worth more than others.
Mount · Side control · Back control · Guard
Week 03
The Guard — Fighting From Your Back
Most people assume being on your back means you’re losing. In BJJ, a closed guard is a weapon. This week introduces guard retention, posture breaking, and why the bottom position is not a last resort.
Closed guard · Posture control · Hip movement
Week 04
Your First Submission — The Rear Naked Choke
The most important choke in BJJ, and the clearest lesson in why leverage beats strength. Anatomy of the choke, how to establish the back position, and how to finish without muscling it. First live drilling with resistance.
First live drilling
Week 05
Escapes — Getting Out of Bad Positions
You will end up on the bottom. This week is entirely about what to do when that happens. Bridge and roll from mount, elbow-knee escape, and why survival is a legitimate strategy. You don’t always have to win — sometimes not losing is enough.
Mount escape · Side control escape · Bridge & roll
Week 06
Takedowns — How the Fight Goes to the Ground
Most BJJ happens on the mat, but it starts standing. Introduction to basic takedowns and trips that are safe for adult bodies — no high-amplitude throws. How to enter the clinch, control distance, and finish safely.
Single leg entry · Body lock trip · Clinch control
Week 07
Putting It Together — Your First Roll
This is the week everything becomes live. Full rolling with partners from within the cohort, with coaching pauses and specific problem-solving. No thrown-in-at-the-deep-end moment — you’ve had six weeks to prepare for this. Debrief on what you noticed.
Full cohort rolling
Week 08
What Comes Next — Transition to Regular Training
Final session is half rolling, half conversation. What regular Monday and Thursday group classes look like. What to expect in your first month as a full member. Individual coaching notes for each student. A practical map for where your game goes from here.
Rolling · Individual debrief · Next steps

Questions
Do I need any gear before the first session?
No gi required for the course — all 8 weeks are conducted no-gi. You need a rash guard or compression top, training shorts or leggings, and a mouthguard by week 4 when live drilling begins. A gear guide is in the Student Manual. If you’re unsure what to buy, ask before you order.
What if I miss a session?
Life happens. If you miss one session, you can catch up on the technical content in the following week. Missing more than two sessions significantly disrupts continuity — the curriculum builds week on week and does not loop back. If a scheduling conflict looks likely, raise it before you register.
What happens after the 8 weeks?
Week 8 includes an individual debrief and a practical handoff to regular Monday/Thursday group classes. Foundations graduates have priority access to group class spots. There’s no pressure to continue — but the 8 weeks are designed to make continuing feel like the obvious next step rather than starting over.
Is this the same as a regular VCBJJ class?
No. Regular Monday and Thursday group classes mix belt levels and assume some familiarity with the environment. The Foundations course is slower, more structured, and deliberately separate from the regular timetable. It’s a different room with a different job.
I’m not sure if I’m fit enough. Should I wait?
No. The course is not a fitness programme and does not require a baseline fitness level. You will be tired. You will be uncomfortable. That is different from needing to get fit first. People who wait until they’re fit enough tend to wait indefinitely. Show up as you are.
