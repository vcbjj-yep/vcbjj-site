Phase 5 ✓ Covered Finishing & Technical Precision — D'Arce Choke Troubleshooting

Your most recent PT session (Session #90, 2 Apr 2026) worked specifically on a technique that kept failing — not because you don't know it, but because of a body mechanics error that was quietly undermining it:

  • D'Arce choke when partner turtles — two entry windows identified
  • Window 1 (mid-transition): as partner reaches for far knee — easier entry, one shoulder exposed. This worked well once reviewed.
  • Window 2 (full turtle): the error was attacking neck-to-armpit as default habit — this removed chest pressure and relied on arm strength alone. Partner could easily disconnect your arms.
  • Correction: thumb connecting to back of partner's neck for correct arm placement. Use your leg to counter the arm post while keeping chest-to-back connection → fall to the side → choke.
Key correction to carry: If your chest is not on their back, your arm strength will always be insufficient. The choke is powered by your body weight, not your arms. This applies to all upper body choke attacks from behind — not just the D'Arce.

Why This Follows Session #93
In Session #93 you built the crucifix attack with Daryl on the receiving end. This session reversed it — you worked the escapes and the attack chain available to the bottom player. The dual perspective is deliberate: understanding how a position feels to be trapped in tells you exactly what pressure to maintain as the top player and which windows to close. Both sessions are the same position, seen from both sides.
Escape — Bridge to Elbow Escape
When pinned from top with far underhook, head lifted, and near arm trapped by legs — bridge to create space, then escape the elbow. The bridge does not need to be large; it needs to be timed to when the top player's weight shifts. That moment is the window.
Top Player Counter — Hip Switch to Reverse Scarf
From crucifix top, counter the bridge escape by switching hips into reverse scarf hold. This closes the escape window and maintains control — from reverse scarf, you can hook the far leg to mount or backstep into saddle. The top player's job: read the bridge as it starts, not after it completes.
Arm Saddle Entry from Crucifix Bottom
When caught in crucifix bottom and the top player's arm position opens the opportunity — enter arm saddle. This is the counter-attack from a position you previously had no offensive answer for. The arm saddle entry is the bottom player's version of reading orphan limbs: the top player's arm, reaching to maintain control, becomes the target.
Arm Saddle → Butterfly Guard → Sweep → N/S Choke
From arm saddle, transition into butterfly guard and execute the sweep. After completing the sweep to top position — N/S choke is the #1 priority finish. The chain: arm saddle entry → butterfly transition → sweep timing → N/S choke. Each step flows from the previous one if the connections are maintained through the transition.
What this session adds to your game: You now have an offensive answer from crucifix bottom — a position that previously had no exit other than survival. Arm saddle to butterfly to N/S choke is a complete three-step chain from a disadvantaged position. Give it live rolling attention before the mechanics become muscle memory.
