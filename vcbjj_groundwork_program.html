Before You Begin

Who This
Is For

This programme is written for adults over 40 who train BJJ as a serious hobby — for health, personal growth, and the challenge of learning something difficult. You are not preparing for competition. You are preparing your body to learn without breaking.

If you feel stiff getting onto the mat, if your shoulders ache the morning after training, if you gas out halfway through a roll — this programme addresses those things directly.

◎
Illustration: The Five Pillars Overview
Diagram showing five pillars as interconnected foundations beneath a silhouette in BJJ guard. Pillars labelled. Visual metaphor: building blocks supporting the body.
Medical Clearance

If you have existing injuries, joint replacements, cardiovascular conditions, or haven't exercised in more than 6 months, get clearance from your doctor before starting.

How to Use This Programme

The programme runs 4 days per week, each session lasting 20–35 minutes. You can do it at home — a yoga mat, a resistance band, and a wall.

◎
Illustration: 4-Week Progression Arc
Timeline: W1 (Suppleness/Mobility) → W2 (Structure/Movement) → W3 (Movement/Conditioning) → W4 (all integrated). Duration grows: 20→25→30→35 min. Stacked bar or gradient band.
The Rule

If it hurts, stop. Discomfort is fine. Pain is a signal. There is an important difference between "this is hard" and "something is wrong."

Equipment

Required: Yoga mat, light resistance band (loop), wall space.
Optional: Tennis/lacrosse ball, foam roller, yoga block.

The Foundation

Five Pillars of
Physical Readiness

Every exercise maps to one or more of these pillars. They are the physical attributes that make BJJ technique possible. Click any pillar to expand.

1
SUPPLENESS
Restore softness and elasticity. Stiff bodies break.

Suppleness is not flexibility. Flexibility is a measurement. Suppleness is a quality — how soft, elastic, and resilient your tissues feel under load. A supple body absorbs force. A stiff body transfers it into joints.

◎
Illustration: Supple vs Stiff Tissue
Side-by-side: supple tissue bending under load and returning vs stiff tissue cracking. Like a green branch vs a dry one.

Key areas for BJJ

  • Hip flexors and adductors (guard, shrimping)
  • Thoracic spine (rotational escapes)
  • Shoulders and neck (pressure from pins)
  • Ankles and wrists (base, posting)
2
STRUCTURE
Hold shapes under load. The strength that withstands pressure.

Structure is the ability to hold a position while someone tries to collapse it. Isometric resilience.

◎
Illustration: Structure Shapes → BJJ Positions
Three figures: plank (= frame), glute bridge (= upa), wall sit (= combat base). Arrows connecting exercise to BJJ equivalent.

Muscle mass declines 3–8% per decade after 30. Isometric holds that mirror BJJ shapes are the intervention.

3
MOBILITY
Full, pain-free range of motion in every joint.

Mobility is flexibility plus control. Strong and stable at end range.

◎
Illustration: BJJ Mobility Checklist
Figure in four test positions: cross-legged sit, deep squat, torso rotation, hands-behind-back reach. Green/amber marks.
  • Hips: Cross-legged without pain? Squat below parallel?
  • Spine: Rotate 45° each way?
  • Shoulders: Hands behind back touch?
  • Ankles: Kneel without discomfort?
4
MOVEMENT
Foundational patterns that unlock grappling techniques.

The basic motor patterns BJJ is built on — the movement vocabulary that makes techniques possible.

◎
Illustration: Ground Movement Vocabulary
Sequence strip: hip escape → bridge → technical stand-up → bear crawl → shoulder roll. Comic-strip panels with motion lines.
  • Hip escape: Every escape and guard recovery
  • Bridge: Space from bottom mount/side control
  • Technical stand-up: Floor to standing safely
  • Crawling: Ground-based coordination
  • Rolling: Safe tumbling
5
CONDITIONING
An engine that survives training without crashing.

An energy system matching grappling: short bursts followed by active recovery.

◎
Illustration: BJJ Energy System Demands
Timeline of a 5-min roll: effort spikes during sweep attempts and escapes, recovery during holds. Aerobic/anaerobic zones annotated.
  • Weeks 1–2: Aerobic base — controlled breathing during movement
  • Weeks 3–4: Work-rest intervals mirroring a 5-minute roll
The Programme

Four Weeks.
Four Days Each.

Each session is 20–35 minutes. No gym required. Sessions labelled A, B, C, D — do them in order with at least one rest day between.

W1
LOOSEN & LEARN
Suppleness + Mobility · 4 sessions · 20 min each
+

Discover where your body is tight, restricted, or unfamiliar with the floor. You're mapping your body.

Day A · 20 min
Floor Introduction & Hip Opening
  • 🫁
    EX-01
    Diaphragmatic breathing — lying on back, knees bent, hands on ribs. Breathe into sides of ribcage. Suppleness5 breaths × 3
  • ↕
    EX-02
    Supine pelvic tilts — flatten lower back to mat, then arch away. Slow. Mobility10 reps × 2
  • ◇
    EX-03
    Supine knee drops — knees bent, feet together, let knees fall each side. Suppleness8 each side
  • ⬡
    EX-04
    90/90 hip sit — both legs at 90°, rock between sides. Mobility5 each side × 2
  • △
    EX-05
    Supine figure-4 stretch — ankle over knee, pull toward chest. Suppleness30s each × 2
  • ⌓
    EX-06
    Child's pose — sit back on heels, arms extended, breathe into lower back. Suppleness60s hold
Finish lying on your back 60s. Observe what felt tight. That's your map.

Guidelines

Scaling &
Modifications

◎
Illustration: Scaling Ladder
Three-tier: each exercise at Easier → Standard → Harder. Push-up wall → knees → full. Squat chair → bodyweight → with pause. Step diagram.

Can't do a movement at all

Reduce the range. Meet your body where it is.

Something causes pain

Stop. Substitute the closest pain-free alternative.

Programme feels too easy

Increase hold times 50%. Slow everything down.

Knee, shoulder, or back issues

Knees: reduce depth. Shoulders: below shoulder height. Backs: cat-cow, pelvic tilts, strict planks.

Guidelines

Recovery &
Listening

◎
Illustration: Readiness Self-Check
1–10 gauge: 1–3 red "Rest", 4–5 amber "Mobility only", 6–10 green "Full session".

After 40, recovery is where adaptation happens.

Sleep

7–9 hours. Non-negotiable.

Hydration

Drink throughout the day. Dark urine = behind.

Soreness vs injury

DOMS = general ache, peaks 24–48h. Injury = sharp, joint-localised, worsens with movement.

Self-check

Before each session: "1–10, how ready?" Below 5 → mobility only. Below 3 → rest.

Guidelines

Blending With
Your Classes

◎
Illustration: Weekly Schedule Templates
Three calendars: (1) BJJ 2×/week + 3 sessions, (2) BJJ 3×/week + 2 sessions, (3) No BJJ — full 4. Gold=BJJ, grey=programme, white=rest.

BJJ 2×/week

3 programme sessions. Drop Day D. Programme on non-BJJ days.

BJJ 3×/week

2 sessions. Prioritise Day A + Day C. Use suppleness work as pre-class warm-up.

Not started BJJ yet

Complete the full 4 weeks first.

After the 4 weeks

Repeat with increased hold times, more rounds, faster tempos. Keep suppleness/mobility as a permanent 10–15 min pre-class routine.
